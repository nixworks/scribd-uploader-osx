Sharing private files with another trusted person

If you upload a document privately (see Uploading files that only I can see), other people won’t be able to view the document unless they have its web address (URL). The web address is not something easily guessed. This is called “security through obscurity.”

Normally, when you upload a document to Scribd, it gets a URL like http://www.scribd.com/doc/123-document-title. In order to prevent someone from guessing the URL of your private document, however, private documents get a URL like http://www.scribd.com/doc/123-document-title?secret_password=f9u2c12193mqog8. The string of letters and numbers at the end is chosen randomly and makes the URL difficult to guess. If that string is left out or is incorrect, the document is not shown.

Therefore, if you want someone to view your document, simply give them the URL. It will include the correct string of letters and numbers, and they will be able to view it. Don’t paste the URL in a public place, and don’t give it to people whom you don’t want looking at your private document.

To share a private document with a friend:

  1. Log in to the Scribd.com website.

    Visit www.scribd.com in your browser and click Log In in the upper right-hand corner of the web page. For more information, read How to view files you’ve uploaded.

  2. Click the My Docs link. (It’s the second link on the toolbar that runs along the top of the web page, below the Scribd logo.)

    You will see a list of the documents you uploaded.

  3. Click on a document title to view it.

  4. Click the Share This button above the document viewer pane.

  5. Copy the URL in the text field that appears, then paste it into a document, email, or other message to be sent to your trusted party.

    You can also use the Send This button, next to the Share This button, to have Scribd email your trusted party a link to the document.

Be sure to let your trusted party know that the URL is a secret and should not be disseminated.
